Reflective Essay Rubric Assessment criteria for your reflective essay

Criteria Excellent Good Fair Poor Score
Analysis and Depth of Reflection (15 pts) Demonstrates excellent analytical skills; interprets artworks and experiences with depth and originality; insightful and thoughtful (15 pts) Clear reflection with some critical insights; shows understanding of course concepts (12–14 pts) Basic reflection; mostly descriptive with limited analysis (9–11 pts) Minimal analysis in reflection; lacks critical engagement (8 or below)
Cultural and Contextual Awareness (10 pts) Effectively connects personal reflection to cultural, historical, or social contexts of art; strong integration of course concepts (10 pts) Adequate connection to course concepts; makes some connections to cultural or contextual aspects of art (8–9 pts) Minimal connection; vague or general references to art or course learnings (6–7 pts) No attempt to connect to cultural or contextual aspects of the course (5 and below)
Personal Insight and Growth (15 pts) Clearly articulates how the subject influenced personal perspective, values, or appreciation of art (15 pts) Shows some evidence of personal growth or changed perspective (12–14 pts) Minimal evidence of personal insight or growth (9–11 pts) No evidence of personal engagement or growth (8 or below)
Structure and Clarity (10 pts) Essay is well-organized, coherent, and flows logically; ideas are easy to follow; excellent grammar and visuals (10 pts) Organization is clear with minor lapses in grammar; transitions mostly effective; visuals are present (8–9 pts) Some organization but ideas may be repetitive or loosely connected with minimal errors in grammar; included few photos (6–7 pts) Disorganized; ideas unclear and difficult to follow; visuals are not sufficient (5 and below)
